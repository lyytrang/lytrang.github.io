Snail Skeleton

Lý Trang released her first album ‘Snail Skeleton’ in 2018, which captures enchanting sounds with its unique blend of traditional Vietnamese instruments and candid storytelling. ‘Snail Skeleton’ is a testament to Lý Trang’s deep connection with her roots and her unwavering commitment to shedding light on the untold stories of the past. Drawing inspiration from her own hometown (Lạng Sơn) and her visits to various villages in Vietnam, Trang has incorporated field recordings of traditional instruments (flutes, goong, đàn tính, Champa drums) with synthesizers and guitars, where melodies become storytellers and every note carries the weight of untold tales. The album weaves a narrative that delves into the lives of the comfort women, who were once victims during the Second World War. Trang’s exploration encompasses the experiences of Vietnamese, Southeast Asian, and Korean women who survived the atrocities of war and sought solace in the tranquil villages they now call home. Despite their seemingly peaceful existence, their eyes betray the weight of their painful pasts. Yet, they persevere, tending to their plants, cooking nourishing meals, engaging in heartfelt conversations, and finding solace in song.

Trang’s profound empathy for these remarkable women is vivid throughout ‘Snail Skeleton’. Immersed in documentaries and literature that shed light on their stories, the album effortlessly intertwines various layers of emotions, both dark and bright, creating a tapestry of sounds that can resonate, heal, connect, and honor the resilience of those who have endured.
