Syenite

Bandcamp Spotify Apple Music Youtube

Like the way its title brings to mind processes of mineral crystallisation and rock formulation, Lý Trang’s second full-length release ‘Syenite’ accumulates elements from all of her times past, á as well as her first for Subtext Recordings and mixed/mastered by the label’s James Ginzburg. Born in a North-Eastern mountainous province of Vietnam, Trang spent the formative college years in Hanoi, where friendships in underground circles – including a tenure within Ran Cap Duoi Collective – brought new influences and perspectives. By then an emerging multimedia artist, Trang moved to Moscow 11 months before a war broke out in Europe – the restlessness and disillusion of the times seeping their way into the album’s sonic identity, alongside a sense of loss and yearning for home. Between assortments of hazy drones and uncanny MIDI percussions, industrial stylings and interlocking gong-like rhythmic patterns, haunting indigenous woodwinds and dissonant strings, the music navigates its assured course through rich variations of arrangements, textures, moods and sceneries, at times within the span of an individual piece. Rising above the off-kilter instrumentation are passages of ethereal voices and spoken word, traces of Trang’s earliest work as a dream-folk singer-songwriter, marking presence here on the visceral pedal-heavy closer ‘Featherbrain’, or as part of her take on late-Talk Talk minimalism for the nocturnal ‘Under the Arbat Station’. At the spectrum’s other end, ‘Sand Congee’ seems to hark back to the sinister meditative soundscapes of Coil’s moon-musick years, while ‘I’m Ready to Be Any Animal that Whistled’ conjures up pastoral images with both mythical reverence and hinted-at horror.

‘And as I was looking around / It was ending over again and again’ – Lý Trang calmly observes on fifth track ‘And Phosphenes’, searching for reason as new surroundings erode around her, with hollow rhythms following folkloric instrumental phrases. Rooted in alienation and conceived as an attempt to make sense of all that’s happening around her, the album may or may not have offered the answers, yet it signifies the artistic coming-of-age of a promising talent.
